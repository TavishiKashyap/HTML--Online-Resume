My experience as Creativity Head

I joined the Student Alumni Interaction Cell (SAIC) during my first year of college, eager to explore opportunities to enhance my leadership skills and contribute to my institution’s vibrant community. SAIC’s first major event, Exordium, was fast approaching, and the Executive Board invited applications for various leadership roles, including positions such as Creativity Head, Publicity Head, OEC, etc.
Driven by my enthusiasm for creativity and design, I applied for the position of Creativity Head. After a rigorous selection process involving interviews and portfolio evaluations, I was honored to be appointed to this role. The opportunity to lead a team and contribute creatively to a significant event in my very first semester was both exciting and humbling.
As Creativity Head, my primary responsibility was to oversee the conceptualization and execution of all creative elements for Exordium. This included designing promotional materials, event decor, and thematic elements that aligned with the vision of the event. Leading a diverse team of talented peers, I quickly realized the importance of effective communication, delegation, and teamwork.
The journey, however, was not without its challenges. Coordinating with multiple stakeholders while meeting tight deadlines required meticulous planning and adaptability. There were moments of uncertainty and setbacks, such as last-minute design changes or resource constraints, which tested my problem-solving skills and ability to stay composed under pressure. Through these experiences, I learned the value of resilience and the importance of fostering a supportive team environment.
I encouraged open communication within the team, ensuring everyone’s ideas were heard and valued. This collaborative approach not only boosted morale but also led to innovative solutions that enhanced the overall impact of the event. Additionally, I worked closely with other department heads, which strengthened my interpersonal and coordination skills.
The event's success was a testament to the collective effort and dedication of the entire SAIC team. Seeing our ideas come to life and witnessing the positive feedback from attendees was incredibly rewarding. It reinforced my belief in the power of teamwork and the significance of strong leadership in achieving shared goals.

My role as Creativity Head at Exordium was a transformative experience that taught me to navigate challenges, lead with empathy, and inspire collaboration. It also deepened my passion for combining creativity with strategic thinking. Moving forward, I aim to leverage these experiences to contribute meaningfully to future projects, both in academic and professional settings, while continuing to grow as a leader and team player.



Following the success of Exordium, I was entrusted with the role of Creativity Head for SAIC’s flagship event, the Global Leadership Summit (GLS). This prestigious event brought together distinguished speakers, industry leaders, and students, making it both an exciting challenge and a significant opportunity for me to further hone my leadership skills.
With prior experience under my belt, I approached this role with greater confidence and a clearer vision. My responsibilities were more expansive this time, as the scale of GLS demanded meticulous attention to detail and a creative strategy that would elevate the event’s impact. From designing the summit’s theme and branding to overseeing the creation of digital and physical assets, I ensured that every creative element aligned seamlessly with the event's objectives.

Having learned the importance of effective teamwork during Exordium, I prioritized building a cohesive and motivated team. I delegated tasks based on individual strengths, fostering a sense of ownership among team members. Regular brainstorming sessions encouraged innovative ideas, while open communication ensured we tackled challenges collaboratively.
Despite our thorough planning, the journey was not without its hurdles. Tight deadlines and unforeseen changes required quick decision-making and adaptability. However, my prior experience helped me stay composed under pressure, and I guided my team to deliver high-quality results.

The highlight of the summit was seeing the event’s branding and design resonate with attendees, creating a memorable experience that reflected our hard work and creativity. The positive feedback from both the organizing committee and participants was deeply gratifying.
My role in GLS solidified my ability to lead large-scale projects, inspire a team, and translate a vision into reality. It was a transformative experience that further enhanced my confidence, problem-solving abilities, and passion for leadership.
